How Recall works

The idea
Recall uses spaced repetition — a technique backed by memory research. Instead of reviewing everything every day, you review each group of cards right before you'd start forgetting them. The harder a card is, the more often you see it.
The 4 piles
Every card lives in one of four piles. When you review a card, you rate how well you knew it — and the card moves to that pile. Each pile has a fixed review schedule:
New Cards you haven't reviewed yet. Always show up in your daily queue.
Hard You barely remembered it. Comes back in 1 day.
Good Solid recall. Comes back in 4 days.
Easy You knew it instantly. Comes back in 10 days.
Batch scheduling
Each pile reviews as a batch — all Hard cards come back together, all Good cards together, all Easy cards together. When you rate a new card, it joins the existing batch for that pile (so it reviews on the same day as other cards already there, not separately). This keeps your review sessions predictable and manageable.
Again
If you couldn't recall a card at all, hit Again. The card goes to the back of your current session so you see it again before finishing — but it doesn't get permanently scheduled until you give it a real rating (Hard/Good/Easy).
Daily habit
Check the Review all due button on the home screen each day. It pulls every card that's due across all your decks into one session. Consistent daily reviews — even just 5 minutes — are far more effective than long cramming sessions.
Adding cards
Create a deck by typing cards manually (term + definition, press ↵ to move between fields) or paste directly from Google Sheets (column A = term, column B = definition — select both, copy, paste).
Archiving
When you finish a class or topic, archive the deck. Archived decks are hidden from your daily review queue but all your progress is saved — you can unarchive anytime.
